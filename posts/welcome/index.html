    IMS211, IMS212#

    My sections of IMS211 and IMS212 are both “hybrid synchronous” courses. For our purposes, that means that you have a choice:

    • Choice A: Twice weekly, at the appointed hour, from your dorm room, your apartment, your RV, or even your pool, you will join us for class. We will meet (typically) via Zoom or Google Meet.

    • Choice B: At the same appointed hour, on the same appointed days, if you wish, you may attend class in-person.

    For the purposes of course requirements and grading, Choice A and Choice B are equivalent and interchangeable. You may stay remote the entire semester without penalty; you may attend in person the entire semester without penalty; you may mix and match without penalty. You do not need to inform me of your choice on a daily basis, nor do you need to tell me about a change in your plans halfway through the semester.

    Online Participation#

    Broadly, then, one last salient point: Given that 2020 was largely something out of a John Carpenter movie, I took a very laissez-faire approach to online participation. Students were not required to appear on-camera during classes, and – not knowing the particulars of anyone’s situation – I was usually loathe to demand students participate in class discussions, etc.. My intentions were good, but the outcomes were not.

    Therefore, as a rule, when you attend class remotely, I fully expect that you will be on-camera and audible for the duration of each class session. To that end, our shared time together this semester is mostly dedicated to (1) in-class discussions, (2) small group work, and (3) occasional student presentations; my lectures and interviews will be available to consume outside of our class meetings.
